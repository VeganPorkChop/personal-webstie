Threaded Items Transfer System

A modular sorting system designed to reduce end-of-year lab cleanup time by mechanically separating mixed fasteners (nuts, bolts, lock nuts, washers, and small hardware) into organized bins.

Planning

Problem

Problem Proposed Solution
At the end of the school year, nuts, bolts, washers, and other small hardware are left unsorted and can take hours to organize. Build a multi-stage machine that separates mixed hardware into labeled buckets with high accuracy and minimal jams.
Goal: Reduce manual sorting time while keeping the system simple to maintain and easy for future students to repair.

Requirements

Essential

  • Sort between the main groups of materials in the lab.
  • Separate tiny hardware (small nuts/bolts), nuts (lock vs standard), washers, and screws/bolts.
  • Maintain consistent throughput with zero jams for essential categories.
  • Achieve ≥ 90% accuracy for essential sorting.
  • Documentation must be complete enough for future repair and troubleshooting.

Non-Essential (Stretch)

  • Separate tiny nuts from tiny bolts.
  • Sort screws/bolts into sizes (M1, M2, M3, M4, etc.).
  • Increase accuracy across all stages beyond minimum requirements.
  • Connect modules to make the system fully automatic (no manual transfer between stages).

Success Statement

  • Accuracy: At least 90% correct sorting for essential categories.
  • Reliability: Zero jams during essential sorting runs.
  • Maintainability: Documentation enables any future student to diagnose and fix failures.

Systems Documentation

Hexagonal Sorter

Each hexagon has a vertical slot cut from the floor up to a specific height. That height corresponds to the maximum size of the part allowed to pass through. Larger parts are caught in higher stages; smaller parts continue downward until the correct stage captures them.

Analogy: This functions like stacked fishing nets—large hardware is caught first, and smaller pieces fall through until they reach the “tight enough” stage. In this system, washers behave like the smallest “fish.”

Troubleshooting & Repairs

Acrylic Cracks
  • If acrylic cracks, replace the panel.
  • The design uses redundant screws, so minor failures should not cause a full system collapse.
Connection Break (Servo Cap / Servo Interface)
  • If the servo cap or connection breaks, reprint the cap.
  • The system uses a 4-way servo horn secured with small bolts to connect to the printed part.
