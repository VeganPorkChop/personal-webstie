Snake AI

A Deep-Q agent that learned to play Snake from an eleven-number view of the world, kept alive by a flood-fill "survival instinct" that refuses to trap itself.

Below you can watch the AI play — or take the controls and see how hard it really is.

Project Overview

Snake AI trains a reinforcement-learning agent to play the arcade classic. A small PyTorch network reads a compact state and picks one of three relative moves — go straight, turn right, or turn left — and a flood-fill algorithm vetoes any choice that would seal the snake into a space too small to escape.

Two brains, one snake

  • The learner — a Deep-Q network that improves by playing thousands of games and remembering what led to food versus death.
  • The guardian — a deterministic flood-fill "survival mask" that runs before every move so the network never has to learn the hard lesson "don't box yourself in".

How the AI works

The 11-feature state

Rather than raw pixels, the agent sees eleven booleans — cheap to compute and enough to play well:

  • Danger straight / right / left — is the cell ahead of each relative direction a wall or body?
  • Direction — one-hot of the current heading (left / right / up / down).
  • Food direction — is the food to the left, right, up, and/or down of the head?

The live inspector in the demo lights these up as the snake moves. The full project extends the danger sensors two cells out for a little more foresight.

The network

Linear(11 → 256) → ReLU → Dropout
Linear(256 → 256) → ReLU → Dropout
Linear(256 → 3)          # Q-value for straight / right / left

Trained with Q-learning: reward +10 for eating, −10 for dying, and a small nudge toward the food each step.

The survival flood fill

Before each move the agent simulates all three actions and, from each resulting head position, flood-fills the reachable free cells. If that count is smaller than the snake's own length, the move is a trap and gets masked out:

for action in (straight, right, left):
    head' = simulate(action)
    reachable = flood_fill(head')          # BFS over free cells
    safe[action] = reachable >= len(snake) * margin

This is why the snake can weave through tight gaps without dying — the guarantee is algorithmic, not learned. The demo draws that reachable region live so you can see the AI's "escape space" shrink and grow.

Notes

The learner lives in agent.py / model.py, the survival mask in survival.py, a hand-written baseline in simple_agent.py, and watch.py replays any saved model at human speed. Saved runs (model.pth, 107.pth, 85.pth) capture different amounts of training.
